The invastion of the lionfish (Pterois volitans & Pterois miles) poses a major threat to Bermuda's marine ecosystem. The presence of them are likely to cause an economic and environmental harm to the reef ecosystems. Lionfish are indiscriminate predators and opportunistic feeders capable of eating up to 30 times their stomach volume. Lionfish can reduce juvenile fish populations on a reed by nearly 90% in as little as five weeks. With no known predators in our region, their population can increase exponentially.

BDA Lionfish Tracker will assist the scientists track the numbers of lionfish that are caught for their research, as well as share location information with everyone who wants to help cull the population. You will be able to submit your catches (which allows you to keep and extend your lionfish permit for free), have a history of your catches, and be able to see the most populated lionfish sites. This app will be developed and released for free for the betterment of Bermuda's reef ecosystem with your help. Lionfish are safe to eat and actually taste pretty good, but we recommend you taking the lionfish course prior to handling them.

FAQ

Where can I get this app?
You can get the app from the Google Play Store or from the Apple iStore. Alternatively, if you don't have a smartphone, you can access it also from the Ocean Support Foundation website.
Can others see where I have caught my lionfish?
If you pin-point the location accurately enough (rather than using a grid) your catch will be marked on a publically viewable grid so that others can get an idea of where the lionfish are more prevalent
What if I want to adobt a reef to protect?
You can do this in the app. Once you do this, you are committing to check that reef. Make sure you specify the next time you plan to visit the reef. Please note that adobting a portion of the reef does not stop others from visiting your site.
